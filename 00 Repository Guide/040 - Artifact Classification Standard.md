# 1. Purpose

This standard establishes the approved artifact classifications used throughout FlashRides OS.

Its purpose is to ensure every controlled artifact has a clearly defined role, consistent expectations, and an understood relationship to other repository artifacts.

---

# 2. Scope

This standard applies to all controlled artifacts maintained within FlashRides OS.

Every controlled artifact shall be assigned an approved artifact classification.

---

# 3. Classification Principles

Artifact classification exists to:

- establish the purpose of an artifact;
- distinguish requirements from guidance;
- reduce ambiguity;
- improve discoverability;
- support governance;
- simplify repository maintenance; and
- promote consistency throughout FlashRides OS.

An artifact classification defines the purpose of an artifact. It does not determine its ownership, approval authority, publication order, or repository location.

---

# 4. Approved Artifact Classifications

FlashRides OS recognizes the following controlled artifact classifications.

---

## 4.1 README

A README introduces a repository section.

A README shall:

- describe the purpose of the section;
- summarize its contents;
- identify intended users;
- explain how the section should be used; and
- direct readers to the associated INDEX.

A README introduces a section but does not inventory its contents.

---

## 4.2 INDEX

An INDEX provides the authoritative inventory of controlled artifacts within a repository section.

An INDEX shall:

- identify controlled artifacts;
- establish publication order;
- support repository navigation; and
- remain consistent with approved artifact metadata.

---

## 4.3 Guide

A Guide provides explanatory or educational information.

Guides explain concepts, context, organizational philosophy, or recommended approaches.

Guides should improve understanding but shall not establish mandatory organizational requirements unless specifically authorized.

---

## 4.4 Standard

A Standard establishes mandatory organizational requirements.

Standards define the rules that govern repository content and organizational activities.

Requirements within Standards shall use normative language such as:

- shall;
- should;
- may; and
- shall not.

Compliance with approved Standards is mandatory unless an approved exception exists.

---

## 4.5 Policy

A Policy establishes organizational direction, intent, or governing principles.

Policies answer the question:

**What has the organization decided?**

Policies generally avoid procedural detail.

---

## 4.6 Procedure

A Procedure defines the approved sequence of activities required to complete a task.

Procedures answer the question:

**How is work performed?**

Procedures should be sufficiently detailed to produce consistent results.

---

## 4.7 Work Instruction

A Work Instruction provides detailed operational guidance for performing a specific activity.

Work Instructions expand upon Procedures where additional detail is necessary.

---

## 4.8 Reference

A Reference provides supporting information.

Reference artifacts may include:

- technical specifications;
- glossaries;
- research;
- external standards;
- templates;
- checklists;
- examples; and
- supporting documentation.

References provide information but do not establish organizational requirements.

---

## 4.9 Record

A Record documents evidence that an activity occurred.

Records preserve historical information and support traceability, compliance, and organizational memory.

Records are not intended to define requirements.

---

## 4.10 Template

A Template provides a standardized structure for creating repository artifacts or organizational documents.

Templates promote consistency while reducing duplication of effort.

---

## 4.11 Form

A Form captures structured information for a defined organizational purpose.

Completed Forms frequently become organizational Records.

---

# 5. Artifact Relationships

Artifact classifications support one another.

Typical relationships include:

- Policies are implemented through Standards.
- Standards are executed through Procedures.
- Procedures may reference Work Instructions.
- References support understanding.
- Templates promote consistency.
- Forms capture information.
- Completed Forms often become Records.

These relationships are informative and do not prevent other valid relationships where appropriate.

---

# 6. Classification Selection

The selected artifact classification shall reflect the artifact's primary purpose.

Artifacts shall not attempt to perform multiple unrelated roles.

Where multiple purposes exist, separate artifacts should normally be created.

---

# 7. Classification Changes

An approved artifact may be reclassified where organizational needs change.

Classification changes shall be approved through the applicable repository governance process.

---

# 8. Exceptions

Exceptions to this standard require approval by Repository Governance.

Approved exceptions shall be documented.

---

# 9. Compliance

Repository contributors and reviewers shall ensure every controlled artifact has an approved classification before approval.

---

# 10. Relationship to Other Repository Artifacts

# 11. Maintenance

This standard shall be maintained by Repository Governance.

It shall be reviewed whenever new artifact classifications are introduced or existing classifications are revised.
